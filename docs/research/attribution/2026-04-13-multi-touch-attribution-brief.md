# Research Brief: Multi-Touch Attribution Analysis

**Date**: 2026-04-13  
**Confidence Level**: High  
**Author**: Growth Revenue Department  

## Topic

Multi-touch attribution models for customer journeys with 3+ touchpoints

## Key Findings

1. **Linear attribution** provides equal credit across all touchpoints
   - Works well when all channels contribute equally
   - May undervalue high-impact early touches

2. **Time-decay attribution** gives more credit to recent touchpoints
   - Good for consideration-heavy products
   - May undervalue awareness-building channels

3. **U-shaped attribution** (40% first, 20% middle, 40% last)
   - Balances first-touch awareness and last-touch conversion
   - Best for moderate-length journeys (5-10 touches)

4. **Position-based attribution** allows configurable weights
   - Flexible but requires tuning
   - Risk of overfitting to historical data

## Industry Benchmarks

| Model | Typical Accuracy | Best For |
|-------|------------------|----------|
| Linear | 60-70% | Equal contribution |
| Time Decay | 65-75% | Short consideration cycles |
| U-Shaped | 70-80% | Balanced journeys |
| Data-Driven | 75-85% | Large datasets |
