# Research Brief: Experiment Learning Archive

**Date**: 2026-04-13  
**Confidence Level**: High  
**Author**: Growth Revenue Department  

## Topic

Patterns and learnings from growth experiments

## Key Patterns from Historical Experiments

1. **High-impact, low-effort experiments** succeed most often
   - CTA button color changes
   - Headline swaps
   - Form field reductions

2. **Traffic-dependent experiments** need sufficient sample
   - Minimum 1000 visitors per variant for reliable results
   - Account for weekend/weekday variation

3. **Multivariate tests** require larger samples
   - 3+ variants multiply sample requirements
   - Consider sequential testing instead

## Experiment Success Criteria

| Metric | Threshold | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Statistical Significance | p < 0.05 | Primary gate |
| Minimum Sample | 1000/variant | Prevents noise |
| Effect Size | >5% lift | Practical significance |
| Revenue Impact | >$X | Business-dependent |

## Learning Reuse

Successful experiments should:
1. Document hypothesis and findings
2. Identify applicable segments
3. Create playbook entry
4. Track as pattern for future reference
